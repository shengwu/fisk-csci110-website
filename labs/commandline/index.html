Lab 12: Command line

The Unix command line has a bunch of incredibly powerful utilities. The philosophy is having individual tools that are very good at their jobs. One example: with dig, you can query Wikipedia from your command line.

However, much of the power comes from pipes. A pipe (|) takes the output from one command and uses it as input to the next. Here's a ridiculous example that outputs your computer's microphone to another computer's speaker.

NOTE: the Windows "Command Prompt" behaves differently than the command lines on Linux and Mac computers. Many of the principles are the same, but for this lab, please use Cygwin on the lab computers, or Terminal/iTerm on a personal Mac or Linux computer.
